  • Dynamo FC

    In 1986 he joined the youth system of Dynamo Kyiv. He helped Dynamo Kyiv to claim Ukrainian Premier League title in 1995, 1996, 1997, 1998 and 1999.

  • Chelsea FC

    Played for Chelsea between 2006 and 2009. Shevchenko signed for Chelsea in May 2006 for a club record £30m, following a decorated club career with Dynamo Kiev and AC Milan.

  • AC Milan

    Andriy Shevchenko arrived at Milanello in 1999, after winning 5 league titles with Dinamo Kiev under the guidance of his mentor Valeriy Lobanovski. Only Nordahl scored for Milan more goals than he did.

Here's a time line of Andriy's life

  • 1976 born 29 September, Dvirkivschyna, Kiev Oblast
  • 1986 after his family relocated to escape the Chernobyl disaster, Shevchenko failed a dribbling test for entrance to a specialist sports school in Kyiv
  • 1990 playing for the Dynamo Kyiv under-14 team in the Ian Rush Cup in Wales, was top scorer in the tournament, and was awarded a pair of Rush's boots as a prize by the Liverpool F.C. player himself
  • 1992-93 he was the top scorer for Dynamo-2 with twelve goals, and he made an appearance in the first team list, taking Dynamo to their second successive league victory
  • 1996 scored his first international goal, in a friendly against Turkey, at Samsun
  • 1997-98 and 1998-99 This two seasons, were abundantly productive for Shevchenko. His 19 goals in 23 league matches, 6 goals in 10 Champions League matches, and another league victory with Dynamo in 1997-98 were followed by 28 goals in all competitions in 1998-99, and the league top-scorer award for his 18 goals there
  • 1999 Shevchenko joined the then five-time European Cup champions A.C. Milan for $25 million, breaking the club's transfer record (it was later broken by Manuel Rui Costa in 2001) and instantly became one of their top players
  • 2006 Shevchenko made his debut for Chelsea on August 13 in the FA Community Shield, scoring his side's goal in a 2-1 loss to Liverpool.
